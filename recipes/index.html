Airtable Project: Recipe & Ingredient Database

Project Overview

Objectives

This is a database I built for myself to collect and organize the recipes I want to cook throughout the week!

I wanted to streamline my grocery shopping so that I could tell at a glance what I need to get for the week ahead.

Outcomes

Where I used to spend hours trying to figure out everything I need for different recipes and making a new list every week, now I spend 30 minutes marking off the recipes I want and checking the fridge for what's gone bad, and I'm all set!

In the store, I have a ready-made shopping list on the mobile app that is already organized by aisle.

Schema Design

Recipes

This table stores recipes, their ingredients, optional substitutions, and links to full recipes where applicable.

I also have a specific view for recipes that use leftover ingredients, or ingredients I already have in the kitchen!

View Recipes (New Tab)

Ingredients

This table stores all of the ingredients used in the Recipes table, the recipes that need them, whether I have them in the kitchen already, and the aisle they are found in-store.

View Ingredients (New Tab)

Workflow

Choose Meals for the Week

Every Sunday, I go through the list of Recipes and check off the ones I want to cook that week.

Confirm Ingredient Status

In the Ingredients list, I have a specific view that pulls up all the ingredients I need for the week's recipes and sorts them by type. This way I can make sure to check the fresh veggies carefully to see if the leftovers in the fridge are still good, but I can skim through the stable pantry section as those items won't have gone bad.

In-Store Shopping List

Once I have confirmed the status of needed ingredients, I have another view that shows all the ingredients that are either running low or completely out of so that I can get them easily at the store.

I've also grouped this view by aisle so that I don't have to backtrack at the store.

Challenges and Solutions

The two-dimensional relational database is great for the simplicity it allows between the recipe and the ingredient tables. However, it does lack the ability to automatically calculate the amount of an ingredient that is needed across all the marked recipes.

I resolve this issue by including a "Minimum needed" field in the Ingredients table, where I note the required amounts for my common recipes. I also include the linked records for the actual recipes in all my shopping list views, so that I can double-check the amounts in the recipe itself when in doubt.

Some other functionalities that I am planning to implement in the future include a rough estimate of expiration for fresh items, based on the date last purchased. I am also considering a price estimator!
