Airtable Project: Grant Editing Service

Project Overview

Objectives

The primary goal of this project was to reduce the administrative burden of applicant tracking for the Grant Editing Service.

Previously, the Editor was managing all logistical aspects of investigators requesting her services, assigning them time slots, sending reminders, and tracking the service manually via email and Excel sheets.

The client's main request was to design a booking system that would allow investigators to book slots autonomously. In the process of meeting with the client to understand her workflow, I identified that I could also improve her workflow for tracking statuses, sending reminders, assessment management, and displaying impact metrics.

Outcomes

Total administrative burden was reduced by 70%, from more than 3 hours / week to less than 1 hour / week.

Late submissions and same-day cancellations were also reduced by 60% after implementing automated reminders and clear policies.

Schema Design

Dates Available

One table manages all the available slots for the editing service. The Editor has access to add her availability whenever she finalizes it, and available slots are synced live to a public calendar.

View Calendar (New Tab)

Editing Requests (Web Form)

Another table manages active requests. A native Airtable form allows investigators to submit requests into this table, reserving the slot immediately on submission.

The form incudes conditional logic allowing investigators to see only the slots that are appropriate for their request.

View Sample Form (New Tab)

Metrics Interface

A customized graphical interface reports out metrics and outcomes collected throughout the editing process, showing the effectiveness of the program at-a-glance.

View Metrics Interface (New Tab)

Workflow Automation

Booking Confirmation & Reminders

When an investigator books a slot, they are emailed a custom confirmation with specific details according to the parameters of their request.

A custom Javascript script parses the details of the request and generates the appropriate content to be added to the confirmation email.

Investigators also receive a reminder to be prepared to submit on time the following Monday at noon.

This system reduced late submissions and same-day cancellations by 60%.

Status Tracking & At-A-Glance Digests

The grant editor receives an emailed digest every week with upcoming slots. She is able to manipulate those slots (cancel, reschedule, promote from waitlist) directly from her email without needing to navigate through multiple systems, and all changes are immediately pushed to Airtable.

In the same email, the editor is prompted to record whether she completed the previous week's editing, so she never has to worry about tracking them herself.

The client and I identified that this email-focused system would better meet her needs than an interface within Airtable itself, so I designed custom automations and forms to make it happen according to her specifications.

Feedback Surveys

2 weeks after receiving edits, when they have had a chance to review them thoroughly, investigators are sent a personal feedback survey about the service's quality.

1 year after receiving edits, when they are likely to have submitted the grant and heard results, investigators are sent a brief outcomes survey to record the success grants that was edited.

Challenges and Solutions

I designed the bulk of this custom Airtable solution in 2021. A lot of the modern functionality of Airtable that allows for robust customization of forms, interfaces, and especially editing existing records did not yet exist.

I created workarounds that achieved the technical requirements of the grant editor client, but some of those solutions were less elegant than others. Figuring out how to do things "the hard way" before those easy features were released was difficult and slowed down the development process, but it also gave me a great understanding of the ins and outs of the Airtable platform, particularly writing custom Javascript code to extend functionality where necessary.

In particular, recent Airtable updates have introduced a swell of tools improving the options for Interface design to report on metrics. Some of these I have already integrated into the existing database to great effect, while I can see that others would be even more effective when designed intentionally into a system from the beginning. I am excited to bring my knowledge of these tools forwards to future projects.
